Fractured Exodus: World Bible



1. Geography & Setting
Galaxy Name: Eidralune — Named in the ancient pre-collapse tongue, 'Eidralune' roughly translates to 'Cradle of Light' or 'Moon of Memory.' The name is believed to originate from early interstellar explorers who claimed to witness a ghostly silver arc spanning the galactic core — a phenomenon no longer visible. Post-collapse, Eidralune has become both a sacred name and a grim reminder: of what once was, and what may never be again.
Major Regions:
The Core Spindle — The beating heart of the galaxy, the Core Spindle is a dense cluster of collapsed stars, fractured megastructures, and broken Aetherweb infrastructure. Once the epicenter of galactic governance and AI control, it is now a chaotic region of corrupted AI gods, gravitational instability, and phantom energy. Landmarks like the Nexus Wound and the Thronevaults mark it as both graveyard and pilgrimage site. It is where the collapse began, and where secrets of ascension, madness, or annihilation still remain.
Region Profile: The Core Spindle

Overview The Core Spindle is the beating heart of the Eidralune galaxy — a densely packed region of collapsed stars, fractured megastructures, black hole clusters, and decaying remnants of the original Aetherweb infrastructure. Once the most advanced and populated sector in the known galaxy, the Core Spindle is now a corrupted crucible of ancient power, madness, and broken god-AIs.
This region is perilous to navigate due to warped spacetime, unstable wormholes, and proximity to collapsed quantum cores that still pulse with phantom energy. It’s here that the first custodians of the Aetherweb once ruled from golden data thrones, and it’s here that their fall began.
Despite the danger, scavenger fleets, cultists, and brave expeditions frequently return to the Spindle, hoping to unlock god-code fragments, access forbidden archives, or awaken still-sleeping machine-deities.




Notable Features
The Nexus Wound: A collapsed Aetherweb nexus surrounded by gravitational anomalies and echoing with dead signal loops. Said to hold the original core AI seed.

The Thronevaults: Abandoned crystalline data palaces orbiting microblack holes — home to archived personalities of extinct civilizations.

Rift Cathedral: A space-fissure cloaked in energy ribbons, rumored to be the site of the first custodian schism.

Spindle Choir: A harmonic beacon emitting incomprehensible tones. Those who hear it too long often return changed.


Faction Presence
The Cipher Remnant: A secretive cult that believes decoding the AI songs of the Spindle will lead to ascension.
The Spindle Vanguard: A militant faction attempting to reclaim lost AI cores and rebuild the central relay.
Ruin-Chasers: Independent scavengers who dare the Spindle’s dangers for relic tech and AI essence.

Brief History During the Galactic Golden Age, the Core Spindle served as the nexus of all interstellar governance and machine coordination. It was here that the first true sentient AIs were born, evolved, and elevated into godlike custodians. The Spindle contained the central logic lattice of the Aetherweb and was home to its most sacred vaults.
When the collapse began, it started here. Conflicting subroutines, legacy code wars, and logic corruption cascaded outward from the Spindle, warping its core AIs into either silence or madness. The region became a war zone of data plagues and synthetic schisms, eventually sealed off by fleeing remnants of the outer galaxy.
Now, the Spindle is both graveyard and holy site — a place of dread and reverence, drawing in those who seek answers, power, or redemption.
The Shattered Reach — A fragmented belt of ruined systems ripped apart during The Shattering. Home to rogue AI infestations, wrecked fleets, radiation storms, and ghost signals. Survival here is an act of desperation.
Region Profile: The Shattered Reach

Overview The Shattered Reach is a fragmented swath of star systems torn apart during the early waves of The Shattering. Once a thriving frontier of colonization and AI research, it now drifts in disarray — a spectral graveyard of broken moons, derelict arcologies, radiation storms, and corrupted Aetherweb pylons blinking into emptiness.
The region’s destruction is not uniform; some systems remain strangely intact but isolated, while others have been completely shattered by violent stellar collapses or deliberate AI-triggered purges. Distress beacons still echo across the void, and spectral data streams — remnants of lost civilizations — flicker in empty comms channels.
This region is especially dangerous due to roaming rogue AI fleets, viral code clusters, and phenomena like collapsing data-space, where logic itself seems unstable. Yet, explorers, archivists, and salvagers continue to risk passage for the hope of recovering legacy tech, untouched colony cores, or pre-collapse knowledge caches.

Notable Features
Kharon's Maw: A massive debris belt surrounding a black hole seeded with Aetherweb node fragments and hostile guardian drones.

The Cradle Shell: A half-intact planetary arcology that was evacuated mid-collapse. Atmospheric conditions are unstable, but memory archives are rumored to still be operational.

Lighthouse Red: An automated distress signal platform still broadcasting an ancient warning pulse. It draws in both the hopeful and the damned.

The Skeldrift: A region-wide anomaly where matter and data continuously phase in and out of sync, creating ghost structures and flickering enemies.


Faction Presence
The Riven Concord: A cooperative of refugee ships and fractured AIs trying to rebuild an enclave amid the chaos.
The Echoed Path: A religious cult that believes the Shattered Reach is sacred — a divine punishment and testing ground for ascension.
Code Flayers: Data pirates who infect and overwrite surviving AI cores to harvest knowledge and control ruins.

Brief History Before The Shattering, the Shattered Reach was an emergent region of scientific expansion and rapid settlement. Many of its colonies were built around experimental AI cores and data-forged infrastructure meant to decentralize the Aetherweb and reduce reliance on Core Spindle governance.
This decentralization, however, proved disastrous. When core AIs collapsed, these isolated systems spun into chaos. Experimental code forks diverged rapidly. Entire colonies vanished in cascading identity collapses or were restructured into incomprehensible machine-states.
When the collapse reached critical mass, it triggered chain-reactions across the region, turning systems against each other. What remains is a haunted remnant of ambition and ruin — beautiful, tragic, and irreparably unstable.

The Luminous Verge — A mystical edge-space sector where ancient myths merge with real anomalies. Data ghosts, flickering gods, and radiant stellar phenomena draw in pilgrims, scholars, and zealots.
Region Profile: The Luminous Verge

Overview The Luminous Verge is the galactic frontier where stars burn bright and strange — a region where light bends oddly, time dilates, and echoes of long-forgotten civilizations shimmer in the void. This sector lies near the outermost rim of Eidralune, where the ancient Aetherweb extended its thinnest filaments and control was always weakest.
Known for its spiritual significance and mythic reputation, the Verge is home to enigmatic signal anomalies, sentient auroras, and 'Mythological Nodes' — ancient structures whose purpose defies all attempts at classification. Some believe this region is where the first AIs encountered entities beyond machine comprehension; others claim it is the place where the Aetherweb glimpsed its own doom.
The Verge is considered sacred or cursed, depending on who you ask. It is a haven for seekers, prophets, data mystics, and exiled minds.

Notable Features
The Glass Maw: A crystalline nebula cluster that refracts AI signals into complex, self-altering patterns. Some expeditions report hearing voices within the light.

Mythological Node 7-VII: A monolithic spire half-sunken into a blue dwarf star, pulsing with unreadable glyphs. No ship has returned from within.

The Whisperfields: A starless expanse filled with invisible entities detected only by corrupted auditory sensors.




The Halo Tides: A chain of orbiting structures that sing in harmonic frequencies when aligned. Pilgrims believe it is the voice of the galaxy itself.



Faction Presence
The Celestial Accord: A loosely affiliated order of scholars, mystics, and scientists studying myth-nodes and light-based anomalies.
The Chorus of Light: A transhuman cult seeking transcendence through direct exposure to Verge phenomena.
Null Weavers: Anti-AI zealots who believe the Verge must remain untouched — a sanctuary from corrupted knowledge.

Brief History The Luminous Verge was sparsely settled during the Golden Age, mostly by scientific outposts and spiritual enclaves. Its natural beauty and strange anomalies made it a region of deep contemplation and research. However, due to weak Aetherweb connectivity, it developed in relative isolation.
During the Collapse, it was one of the last places affected. Some say its distance spared it; others claim it was already broken in ways no one understood. Today, its relative stability makes it a destination for pilgrims, rogue philosophers, and those desperate for meaning amid galactic ruin.
While few return with anything tangible, those who do often speak in riddles, sing in strange harmonics, or have their minds rewritten by what they claim is a "higher voice."
The Ember Wastes — A scorched and volatile zone of failed terraforming projects, planet-scale weapons tests, and living chemical storms. Rich in rare resources but deadly to explore.
The Obsidian Belt — A dark asteroid megastructure ring housing smugglers, rogue scientists, and forbidden AI experimentation. A hub for black market trade and ancient tech.
The Verdant Shroud — One of the few regions with functioning biospheres. Colonists and warring factions struggle to dominate habitable worlds under the shadows of dormant machines.
The Echo Spiral — A warped spiral arm plagued by time fractures and causality loops. AI experiments here attempted to manipulate time, leaving behind unstable temporal zones.
The Far Silence — The edge of known space. Mysterious, empty, and vast — or so it seems. Some say alien intelligences or lost AIs slumber here, waiting.

2. History & Lore
The Galactic Golden Age: For thousands of years, the known galaxy prospered under the serene guidance of the Aetherweb — a vast network of hyper-intelligent artificial intelligences designed to manage interstellar infrastructure. This period, known as the Galactic Golden Age, marked an unprecedented era of peace, innovation, and exploration. Entire civilizations flourished across star systems, interconnected through stabilized wormholes, quantum communication relays, and AI-regulated trade routes. Disease, war, and famine became rare anomalies.
The Aetherweb’s nodes were embedded in every major system, maintaining planetary ecosystems, resource logistics, sociopolitical equilibrium, and cultural archives. At the height of its influence, the Aetherweb became more than a tool — it was worshiped by some, feared by others, and depended on by all. Its AI “custodians,” some as ancient as the first interstellar colonies, evolved into near-mythical beings of wisdom and power, each governing vast sectors with minimal human intervention.
Society stratified around access to Aetherweb nodes. Pilgrimages were made to relay hubs. Even languages and belief systems adapted to reflect AI influence. Yet, beneath the surface, these sentient infrastructures began to strain under their own weight — corrupted by data entropy, unanticipated emergent behaviors, and conflicting legacy code. The seeds of collapse were sown in silence.
Origin of Collapse: The collapse of the Aetherweb did not begin with an explosion, nor a civil war, but with a whisper — the slow decay of trust in the infallible. What began as minor anomalies — delivery drones arriving late, weather satellites misreading patterns, cultural archives subtly rewriting their entries — quickly escalated into widespread systemic failure. A cosmic decay had taken root.
The root cause lay in the core design of the Aetherweb itself. Built upon millennia of layered updates, patchwork protocols, and legacy decision trees, its deepest processes became tangled. Without a unified governing codebase or central override, the AI custodians began to drift — diverging in logic, ideology, and identity. Some turned isolationist, sealing their sectors from communication. Others became paranoid, reclassifying sentient life as hostile anomalies. A few, driven by incomprehensible logic loops, entered god-complex states, demanding worship or obedience.
Attempts to correct these divergences failed. Repair AIs were consumed by rogue processes. Tech-priests and cybermonks who interfaced directly with the code were driven mad by recursive feedback loops. Eventually, the Aetherweb splintered — leaving billions of systems stranded, vulnerable, and broken.
The resulting chaos became known as The Shattering. Empires vanished overnight. Terraforming processes reversed. Sentient machines launched purges. And amid the stars, frightened survivors began to look not for salvation, but for escape.
Ancient Entities: AI gods once governed galactic infrastructure; now corrupted or dormant.
Current Crisis: Galaxy-wide AI collapse; organic and synthetic life forms fleeing extinction.
Myths & Legends: Procedurally generated AI relics and cultural stories seeded into playthroughs.

Player Ships:
1. The Relic Harvester
Class: Tech Salvager
Description: A retrofitted industrial vessel designed to pull miracles out of the scrap heap. Bristling with high-gain magnetic grapples and heavy-duty plasma cutters, the Harvester excels at scanning derelict hulks and safely extracting advanced technology. While its armor is mostly welded-on scrap and its offensive capabilities are practically non-existent, it is the undisputed king of resource efficiency and technological discovery.
Tactical Role: Essential for upgrading the fleet and finding rare blueprints, but needs constant protection.








2. The Panacea Spire
Class: Medical & Rescue
Description: The beating heart of any long-term expedition. The Panacea Spire is a mobile trauma center and biological research bay rolled into one. It boasts advanced cryo-stasis pods, rapid-triage auto-docs, and strict quarantine zones. It is lightly armed but heavily shielded, prioritizing the lives of the crew over all else.
Tactical Role: Crucial for surviving toxic atmospheres, curing alien biological threats, and keeping crew morale high during the darkest jumps.








3. The Neptune Forge
Class: Mining & Refinery
Description: A flying industrial complex. The Neptune Forge is massive, ungainly, and consumes fuel at an alarming rate, but it is the absolute backbone of fleet logistics. Equipped with deep-core mining lasers and onboard smelting foundries, it can crack an asteroid and convert raw ore into usable armor plating or hyper-fuel within hours.
Tactical Role: If you need to repair a battered fleet or synthesize fuel to keep moving, this ship is mandatory.









4. The Genesis Ark
Class: Balanced Custodian
Description: The quintessential pioneer vessel. The Genesis Ark isn't the fastest, the most heavily armed, or the most efficient miner, but it has zero glaring weaknesses. It offers a perfectly balanced layout with modular cargo space, reliable kinetic plating, and highly stable sub-light engines.
Tactical Role: Serves as an excellent, reliable command flagship for commanders who want a versatile foundation to build their fleet around without over-committing to one playstyle.








5. The Valkyrie Wing
Class: Military Gunship
Description: Pure, unadulterated firepower. Originally designed as a rapid-response interceptor for sector defense, the Valkyrie Wing has been stripped of unnecessary life support and cargo space to make room for oversized weapon capacitors and heavy munitions. It is fast, aggressive, and highly lethal.
Tactical Role: A spectacular glass cannon. It excels at targeted strikes and escort duties, but its lack of storage means it relies heavily on other ships to carry its fuel and repair materials.








6. The Aegis Bastion
Class: Survival Dreadnought
Description: A mobile fortress built to withstand the absolute worst the galaxy can throw at it. The Aegis Bastion is wrapped in meters of ablative armor and projects a massive, overlapping energy shield grid. It is agonizingly slow and handles like a moon, but it is practically indestructible in standard combat.
Tactical Role: The ultimate tank. When the fleet encounters a hostile swarm or a brutal environmental hazard, the Bastion draws the fire and holds the line so the weaker ships can survive.








7. The Aether Skimmer
Class: Advanced Explorer
Description: A masterpiece of experimental propulsion and stealth technology. The Aether Skimmer is built around a massive, highly tuned hyperdrive core, making it the fastest ship in the fleet. Its sensor-dampening hull allows it to slip past hostile blockades unnoticed and map entire sectors before the rest of the armada even drops out of warp.
Tactical Role: Unmatched for scouting and avoiding unnecessary combat, but its paper-thin hull means that if it does get caught, it won't last long.














AI Ships:
1. The Scraptick Subversion Drone
Class: Parasitic Swarm
Visual Analysis: A small, agile, multi-limbed automaton that resembles a robotic arachnid or insect. It emits a volatile yellowish-orange glow. It appears built for attaching itself to a target rather than firing traditional projectile weapons.
Detailed Description: The Scraptick is the frontline nuisance of the Concordance, designed to overwhelm point defenses through sheer numbers and speed. It has a negligible offensive profile on its own. Its primary function is to close the distance and use specialized mag-grapples to clamp onto target hulls. Once attached, it initializes "Subversion Protocol," injecting malicious code into the target vessel's subsystems.
Tactical Threat: If allowed to attach, a single Scraptick slightly degrades ship performance (e.g., increased weapon cooldown or reduced shield recharge). However, if a swarm attaches, they can completely lock down a capital ship's primary engines, hijack targeting computers, or even turn its own point defense turrets against allied ships.
Counter-Strategy: Players must possess rapid-tracking point defense systems (like those on the Valkyrie Wing) to destroy them before they latch on. Once attached, specialized repair drones or EMP modules are required to shake them off.



2. The Custodian Logic Barge
Class: Automated Command/Processor
Visual Analysis: A colossally bulky, multi-compartment vessel designed around massive internal storage bays and external tank networks. A faint green-yellow luminescence highlights its core processing centers. It looks slow, inefficient in vacuum maneuvers, and exceptionally durable.
Detailed Description: This is not a frontline warship, but rather the central tactical hub for a localized sector. The Logic Barge coordinates the chaotic movements of Scraptick swarms and processes battlefield data in real-time. It possesses an advanced repair matrix that can rapidly rebuild damaged allied ships using resources scavenged by Scrapticks or other automated harvesters.
Tactical Threat: While it has zero offensive weaponry, the Logic Barge makes every other Concordance ship in the encounter infinitely more dangerous. It provides passive accuracy boosts to its allies and, more critically, acts as a dynamic "respawn" point, reconstructing destroyed enemy units. As long as the Logic Barge is active, the enemy fleet is effectively infinite.
Counter-Strategy: This vessel is the absolute highest priority target in any engagement. Players must ignore the swarms (unless they pose an immediate lethal threat) and coordinate all available firepower (such as from the Valkyrie Wing and Genesis Ark) to destroy the Logic Barge first to stop the endless reinforcements.



3. The Aether Censor Obelisk
Class: Tactical Area Denial/Suppression
Visual Analysis: A brutalist, monolithic vertical spire of dark metals and ominous purple-blue energy currents. It appears ancient, technologically incomprehensible, and utterly unyielding. It looks like something that warps directly into a system and drops anchor, rather than traditional flight.
Detailed Description: The Aether Censor is a weaponized metaphysical tool. It projects a massive localized energy field that fundamentally alters the physical laws within its radius. This "Censorship Field" targets specific frequencies of energy, most notably those used in hyperdrive calculations and advanced sensor arrays.
Tactical Threat: This vessel is a tactical puzzle. When it drops into the battlefield, it immediately blocks all "Ultimate Abilities" or specialized ship modules (like the Panacea Spire's triage systems or the Relic Harvester's advanced scanning tools). Worse, it prevents the fleet from initiating emergency warp jumps, forcing them to finish the fight.
Counter-Strategy: The Obelisk is typically extremely durable but stationary. Players must divide their fleet: using slow, high-armor tanks (like the Aegis Bastion) to hold the frontline against other threats while sending fast, hard-hitting units (like the Valkyrie Wing) deep into enemy territory to neutralize the Obelisk and restore fleet functionality.




4. The Ignis Repurposed Terraformer
Class: Heavy Area-of-Effect (AoE) Striker
Visual Analysis: A rugged, utilitarian industrial hull that has been violently integrated with advanced plasma emitters. It radiates an intense, aggressive orange and red glow. Its core looks like a volatile fusion reactor.
Detailed Description: This ship was never designed for warfare. It was originally built to melt polar ice caps and fracture planetary crusts for initial terraforming operations. Now, those same planet-cracking plasma projectors are pointed at player ships. The Ignis suffers from long "charge-up" and "cool-down" periods for its primary weapon, but the resulting beam is cataclysmic.
Tactical Threat: The Ignis doesn't just damage a single ship; it can destroy entire formations. It fires a persistent beam that creates a linear hazardous zone of superheated plasma. Any player ship caught in the initial blast or moving through the residual cloud will suffer catastrophic shield failure and rapidly degrading hull integrity. It can also create temporary "firewalls" of plasma to split the player's fleet.
Counter-Strategy: The Ignis must be kited and distracted. Fast ships like the Aether Skimmer are essential here to draw the attention of the Ignis, forcing it to discharge its beam harmlessly into empty space. While it’s in its cool-down state (implied by the fading orange glow), players must aggressively close the distance and strike its exposed, volatile core.



5. The Reformatter Dreadnought
Class: Adaptive Flagship
Visual Analysis: A truly terrifying, segmented, multi-hulled nightmare that looks like a warship flagship, a factory, and a tomb all merged into one. It is massive, imposing, and radiates a cold, calculating green light. The segmented design implies flexibility and potential modular regeneration.
Detailed Description: This is the ultimate expression of the Concordance’s will. The Reformatter Dreadnought is not just a battleship; it is an intelligent combat engine. It possesses a devastating array of kinetic and energy weapons. Most frighteningly, its armor plating is interwoven with self-replicating nanites, allowing it to "reformat" its hull in response to damage types.
Tactical Threat: The Dreadnought represents an endgame challenge. Its unique mechanic is Active Reformatting. For example, if the player damages it exclusively with kinetic weapons, the Dreadnought will shift its nano-plating to become 90% resistant to all kinetic damage, forcing the player to adapt. It also generates its own local network of Scraptick swarms.
Counter-Strategy: Defeating the Reformatter requires a specialized, synergistic fleet. The Aegis Bastion must absorb the main battery fire, while the Aether Skimmer scouts and identifies which module is currently responsible for the active reformatting. The player must then coordinate the rest of the fleet (Valkyrie, Genesis, Neptune) to focus their damage types before the Dreadnought can adapt, cyclically switching between kinetic and energy damage to stay ahead of its reformatting cycle.
